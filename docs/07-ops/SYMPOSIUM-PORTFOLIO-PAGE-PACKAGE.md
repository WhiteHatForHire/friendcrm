# Product Overview

Friend CRM is a playable local-first prototype for remembering people thoughtfully. It combines a private relationship notebook, a planning board, source-backed AI workflow shells, and an intentionally absurd retro visual identity.

The product joke is simple: it looks like a suspicious friendship bureau, but the product behavior is built around restraint. No scraping. No automated sending. No hidden scoring. No durable AI-generated memory without review.

## What It Does

- Tracks people, context, relationship labels, contact timing, memories, open loops, and next moves.
- Captures reflection notes and proposes source-backed records for review.
- Generates draft briefs and next-move ideas from known context.
- Gives users a Plot Board for planning follow-through.
- Keeps export, restore, reset, and deletion paths visible.
- Runs as a fake-data demo with deterministic fallback behavior.

## What It Refuses To Do

- Scrape private messages.
- Send automated outreach.
- Rank people with hidden scores.
- Save AI-generated memories without explicit confirmation.
- Hide data ownership behind a black box.

# Section: AI And Privacy

## Heading

AI That Has To Ask Permission

## Copy

The AI direction is intentionally conservative. Friend CRM treats AI as a drafting assistant, not an authority. Suggestions are source-backed, reviewable, and disposable until the user confirms them.

The prototype includes deterministic fallback behavior and development route shells for AI extraction, briefs, and generated next moves. Real provider calls are kept behind a server-side boundary, and the public portfolio story should stay clear: this is a fake-data demo, not a hosted production system for real private data.

## Principles

- The database owns facts; AI proposes structure and language.
- Durable memory requires explicit user confirmation.
- Suggestions should be traceable to user-entered notes.
- Export and deletion must remain visible.
- Public demos should use fake data.

- Deterministic fallback behavior for safe public demos.

# Section: Design Evolution

## Heading

From Sterile Desk To Retro Social Bureau

## Copy

The prototype started as a clean private relationship desk. It worked, but it felt too clinical for the subject matter. The design direction shifted toward a cheekier early-web / fake-bureau identity: louder headings, classified panels, shadowed controls, strange but readable labels, and a tone that admits the product premise is inherently funny.

That visual layer made the product more memorable without loosening the privacy rules. The app can be playful because the boundaries are serious.

## Pull Quote

The product looks like it might be creepy, then refuses to do the creepy stuff.

# Section: Postmortem

## Heading

What This Prototype Taught Us

## Copy

Friend CRM works best when it treats the uncomfortable part of the premise as material, not as something to hide. Remembering people can be thoughtful. Tracking people can be creepy. The product has to live in that tension.

The design breakthrough was making the interface funny enough to acknowledge the tension while making the product rules strict enough to earn trust. Review flows mattered more than generation quality. Plain privacy copy mattered more than cleverness. Browser testing mattered because visual prototypes break in ways unit tests cannot see.

## Lessons

- Humor can make sensitive software feel less sterile, but trust language still has to be direct.
- AI memory should be reviewed, source-backed, and user-confirmed.
- Local-first behavior is a strong default for a personal-data prototype.
- Responsive QA matters when the product is meant to feel like a usable object, not just a mockup.
- A strong portfolio piece needs both personality and restraint.
