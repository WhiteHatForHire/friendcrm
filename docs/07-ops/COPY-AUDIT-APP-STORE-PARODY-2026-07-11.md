# COPY-AUDIT-APP-STORE-PARODY-2026-07-11.md

## Purpose

Audit Friend CRM's visible product copy and propose a stronger, funnier, more original parody voice that still stays App Store-safe.

This is a proposal document. It does not implement copy changes.

## Working Position

Friend CRM should feel like a tongue-in-cheek private relationship desk: bureaucratic, retro, slightly absurd, self-aware, and useful.

The comedy should punch at the product's own over-organization, not at real people. The safest target is the fake institution: "the bureau," "paperwork," "receipts," "soft schemes," "future-you," and "official file." The riskiest target is anything that sounds like stalking, surveillance, coercion, humiliation, or non-consensual monitoring.

## App Store Guardrails

Apple's current App Review Guidelines matter here because the product intentionally plays with "creepy friend CRM" energy.

Relevant guardrails:

- Apple frames App Review around safety, quality, privacy, and clear functionality: https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/
- Safety guidance warns against offensive, upsetting, or "plain creepy" content. Satire can exist, but the line still matters.
- App completeness and metadata must be accurate, tested, and non-placeholder. Reviewers need full access or a full demo mode.
- Copy and metadata must accurately reflect the core experience and avoid hidden or undocumented features.
- Design rules warn against copycats and require apps to provide useful, unique, app-like value.
- Privacy rules require a privacy policy, data minimization, clear disclosure, user consent, and no compilation of personal info from sources the user did not provide or explicitly consent to.

Practical implication: keep the joke clear, but keep the product promise boringly honest. Say "user-entered," "local," "no scraping," "no automated outreach," and "you approve durable records" in the right places.

## Voice Rules

### Keep

- Bureaucratic absurdity.
- Fake paperwork language.
- "Future-you" accountability.
- Explicit privacy assurances.
- Self-aware disclaimers.
- Jokes about over-planning, awkwardness, and local-only records.

### Reduce

- "Suspect" as a primary add-person label.
- "Snooping" as a loading state.
- "Interrogate" as the main save action.
- Anything implying the app secretly watches, tracks, scrapes, or monitors someone.
- Anything that sounds like a real law enforcement claim.

### Avoid

- "Stalker," "spy," "surveillance," "target," "victim," "investigate," "track them," "profile them," "score them."
- Real-world crime language unless the UI clearly marks it as fake paperwork parody.
- Suggestions that automate outreach, scrape socials, judge attractiveness, rate people secretly, or infer hidden traits.

## Recommended Immediate Copy Pass

These are the highest-leverage changes before the next TestFlight/App Store build.

1. Replace "Add a suspect" with "Open a new file" everywhere.
2. Replace "Capture And Interrogate" / "Interrogate Note" with "Save Debrief" or "Save And Review."
3. Replace "Snooping..." / "Re-snoop" with "Assembling..." / "Refresh Brief."
4. Replace "Target File" with "Person File."
5. Replace "Trust the Machine" with "Select All."
6. Replace "Make It Canon" with "Save Approved Records."
7. Replace "Clear Local Evidence" with "Clear Local Data" on App Store-facing mobile builds.
8. Add an App Store-safe local-only disclaimer to the first-run or Evidence screen:

   > Friend CRM stores user-entered relationship notes on this device. It does not scrape messages, contacts, social accounts, or send outreach for you.

9. Keep the fun, but route the edgier language into secondary copy, stamps, and empty states rather than primary action labels.

## Proposed App Store Review Notes Copy

Use something like this in App Store Connect Notes for Review:

> Friend CRM is a local-first, tongue-in-cheek relationship note app for remembering user-entered context about people, promises, boundaries, and next moves. The product uses fake demo data for review and stores records locally on device. It does not scrape contacts, messages, social media, email, or calendar data. It does not automate outreach. Any durable memory or unfinished-business record must be created from user-entered notes and explicitly approved by the user.

If AI/provider features are disabled in the submitted build:

> The submitted build uses local deterministic behavior only. No production AI provider call is required for review.

If AI/provider features are enabled later:

> AI-assisted suggestions are draft-only. They are source-backed to user-entered notes, require explicit approval, and do not send data to third-party AI services without user consent and disclosure.

## Feature Ideas To Make The Product More Original

These are ordered by App Store usefulness and implementation value.

### P0 - Approval-Safe Differentiators

1. First-run "Bureau Oath"

   A short onboarding screen that makes privacy posture explicit:

   - "No scraping."
   - "No automated outreach."
   - "No hidden scoring."
   - "Your notes are local unless you export or enable sync."

   Why it helps: improves reviewer clarity and reinforces originality.

2. Mobile BuddyScan Lite

   Add a mobile-safe fake poster/card generator for a person. It should use only visible profile fields and counts, not private note contents.

   Why it helps: memorable, original, visually portfolio-ready, and clearly comedic.

3. Relationship Receipt

   Replace sterile summary blocks with a "receipt" view:

   - last contact
   - open loops
   - saved debriefs
   - next move
   - privacy label

   Why it helps: useful and on-brand.

4. Review Queue As "Clerk Desk"

   Rename the AI/deterministic review flow into a clerk desk metaphor:

   - "Possible Memory"
   - "Possible Unfinished Business"
   - "Save Approved Records"
   - "Keep Note Only"

   Why it helps: keeps user confirmation clear while giving the app a unique mechanic.

5. Fake Data Tour Mode

   A guided fake-data path for App Review:

   - open Ada
   - save a debrief
   - approve one memory
   - file one next move
   - export local JSON

   Why it helps: reduces review confusion and makes the product feel complete.

### P1 - Fun/Product Depth

1. Socials And Profile Photo

   Let users add LinkedIn, Instagram, website, email, phone, Signal, WhatsApp, and a profile photo. Keep all fields user-entered.

   Safe copy:

   > User-entered links only. Friend CRM does not scrape profiles or import contacts.

2. "Normal Human Bulletin"

   A rotating daily card with harmless prompts:

   - "Send one specific thank-you."
   - "Close one loose thread."
   - "Do not turn brunch into a dashboard."

   Why it helps: fun recurring identity without surveillance.

3. "Fumble Risk" Explanation

   Add a small explanation card:

   - low: simple and warm
   - medium: needs context
   - high: private/sensitive, go carefully

   Why it helps: makes the parody term useful.

4. Saved Copy Snippets

   Allow user-authored next-move drafts to be copied as message text. Do not auto-send.

   Safe copy:

   > Copy draft. You send it yourself, like a legally responsible adult.

5. Easter Egg Stamps

   Add non-functional stamps that rotate on empty states:

   - "NO LORE"
   - "TOO CLEAN"
   - "FILE OPENED"
   - "NOT EVIDENCE"

   Why it helps: gives the product personality without risky functionality.

### P2 - Later, More Ambitious

1. Custom Tone Packs

   Let users choose:

   - Bureau Classic
   - Friendly Plain English
   - Maximum Paperwork

   Why it helps: App Store reviewers can use a clearer mode if the joke feels too loud.

2. Relationship "Case Cards"

   Small printable/shareable local cards with visible fields only. Avoid private note contents by default.

3. Debrief Templates

   User-selectable prompts:

   - "After a call"
   - "After dinner"
   - "After a meeting"
   - "After I promised something again"

4. "Open Loop Amnesty"

   A weekly screen that helps close or drop old unfinished business.

5. Portfolio Demo Mode

   Lock the app into fake data with a visible banner and reset button for public demos.

## Copy Implementation Plan

### Run 1 - App Store Safety Pass

Scope:

- Mobile primary labels and actions.
- App Store-facing status/error copy.
- First-run or Evidence privacy disclaimer.

Acceptance:

- No primary action says "suspect," "snoop," or "interrogate."
- No copy implies scraping, tracking, or secret monitoring.
- App still sounds like Friend CRM.
- `cd apps/mobile && npm run check` passes.
- Local iPhone build passes before TestFlight.

### Run 2 - Web Parity Pass

Scope:

- Web People, PersonRail, ReviewPanel, PlotBoard, SettingsView.

Acceptance:

- Same terminology as mobile where possible.
- Edgier jokes are moved to stamps, helper text, empty states.
- `npm test` and `npm run build` pass.

### Run 3 - Originality Feature Pass

Scope:

- Mobile Relationship Receipt.
- Mobile BuddyScan Lite or first-run Bureau Oath.
- App Store Review Notes draft.

Acceptance:

- Adds one clearly original, app-like experience.
- Keeps all data user-entered/local.
- Does not introduce scraping, automated sending, hidden scoring, or production AI dependency.

## Recommended Priority

Do Run 1 next.

Reason: the current mobile copy has good personality, but "suspect," "interrogate," "snooping," and "target" are avoidable App Store review risks. They are not necessary for the joke. The brand will be stronger if primary UI stays safe and clear while the flavor lives in stamps, helper copy, and fake paperwork.
